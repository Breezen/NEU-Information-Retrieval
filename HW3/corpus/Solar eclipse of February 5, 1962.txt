a total solar eclipse occurred on february 5 1962 a solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between earth and the sun thereby totally or partly obscuring the image of the sun for a viewer on earth a total solar eclipse occurs when the moons apparent diameter is larger than the suns blocking all direct sunlight turning day into darkness totality occurs in a narrow path across earths surface with the partial solar eclipse visible over a surrounding region thousands of kilometres wide totality was visible from indonesia netherlands new guinea now belonging to indonesia the territory of papua new guinea todays papua new guinea british solomon islands todays solomon islands and palmyra atoll
