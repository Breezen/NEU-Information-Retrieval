a total solar eclipse occurred on march 25 1857 a solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between earth and the sun thereby totally or partly obscuring the image of the sun for a viewer on earth a total solar eclipse occurs when the moons apparent diameter is larger than the suns blocking all direct sunlight turning day into darkness totality occurs in a narrow path across earths surface with the partial solar eclipse visible over a surrounding region thousands of kilometres wide totality began at sunrise over southeastern australia on march 26 crossing the pacific ocean and ended near sunset across mexico on march 25
